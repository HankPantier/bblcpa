# Privacy policy

<!-- block: content-prose -->
## Introduction and scope of this policy

This policy covers how Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore CPAs & Consultants collects, uses, and protects information from anyone who visits bblcpa.com, calls our Port Arthur office, or works with us as a client. We know you're trusting us with sensitive financial details, tax records, and payroll data, so we've written this in plain English instead of dense legal boilerplate. It applies to our website, our secure online platform, and the intake process for every service we offer, from bookkeeping to audit protection. If something here doesn't make sense, call us. That's what we're here for.

<!-- block: content-prose -->
## Information we collect when you use our site or services

When you browse bblcpa.com, we collect standard analytics data: pages visited, browser type, and approximate location, gathered through cookies and similar tools. If you submit a contact form or request a consultation, we collect your name, phone number, email, and any business details you choose to share.

Once you become a client, the scope expands to match the service. Outsourced accounting and bookkeeping engagements involve bank and credit card transactions. Payroll services require employee names, wages, and tax identification numbers. Tax preparation, whether business or personal, involves income records, prior returns, and supporting documentation. All of this active client data is collected through secure intake forms or uploaded directly to our online platform, never through unencrypted email when we can help it.

<!-- block: content-prose -->
## How we use your information

We use your information for exactly the work you've hired us to do, nothing more. Payroll data runs payroll and files the related tax forms. Bookkeeping records feed into the financial statements and dashboards available on our secure online platform, giving you real-time visibility into your numbers. Tax information supports preparation, filing, and the year-round planning that's part of our service, not a once-a-year scramble.

For business management clients, we also use financial data to build the budgets, forecasts, and KPI reports that come with CFO-level guidance. Contact form submissions are used only to respond to your inquiry and, if you agree, to send occasional updates relevant to your business.

<!-- block: content-prose -->
## How we protect and secure your data

Financial and payroll data deserves more than a password and good intentions. Our secure online platform uses encryption for data in transit and at rest, and access is restricted to the team members actually working on your account. We don't email sensitive documents as unprotected attachments, and we don't store client records on personal devices.

Internally, staff access is role-based, meaning a bookkeeper doesn't see payroll files for clients outside their assignment, and a payroll specialist doesn't have open access to tax planning notes. We review our platform's security settings regularly and update credentials whenever staffing changes. If you ever want a plain-language explanation of how a specific document is stored, ask your engagement contact directly.

<!-- block: content-prose -->
## Third-party sharing and disclosure

We share client information only where the service requires it. Tax filings go to the IRS and Texas state agencies. Payroll data goes to the payroll processor and relevant tax authorities for deposits and filings. If you're under IRS examination and we're handling your audit protection, we communicate with the IRS on your behalf, with your authorization on file.

We do not sell client data. We do not share your financial records with marketers, data brokers, or unrelated third parties, and we never will. Disclosure outside normal service delivery happens only if required by law or court order.

<!-- block: content-prose -->
## Your rights and how to contact us about this policy

You can ask us at any time what information we hold about you, request a correction, or ask us to delete records we're not legally required to retain (tax documents, for example, often carry mandatory retention periods). You can also opt out of non-essential marketing emails without affecting your service relationship with us.

To make any of these requests, or if you have questions about this policy, reach our Port Arthur team directly. We've answered privacy questions from physicians, restaurant owners, and small business clients across Southeast Texas for decades, and we're glad to walk through ours with you too.

<!-- block: faq-accordion -->
## Frequently Asked Questions About Privacy policy | Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore CPAs & Consultants

**Q: Does Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore sell or share my financial data with marketers?**
A: No. The firm does not sell client data to marketers, data brokers, or unrelated third parties. Information is shared only when a service requires it, such as filing taxes with the IRS or processing payroll deposits, or when required by law.

**Q: How does the firm protect client information on its online platform?**
A: Client data is encrypted in transit and at rest on the secure online platform, with role-based staff access so team members only see records tied to their assigned clients. Security settings are reviewed regularly and credentials are updated as staffing changes.

**Q: Can I ask Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore to delete my information?**
A: You can request deletion of records the firm isn't legally required to retain. Some documents, particularly tax filings, carry mandatory retention periods under federal and state rules, so deletion timing depends on the document type.
