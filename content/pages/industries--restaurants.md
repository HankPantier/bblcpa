## Running a restaurant is hard enough without the bookkeeping headaches

You didn't sign a lease and build a menu so you could spend your nights matching receipts to a bank statement. Restaurants run on thin margins, and a few percentage points in either direction decide whether the month was good or just busy. Tip reporting alone can eat an evening. Labor costs swing with the season, the weather, and who called in sick. Food prices move every week.

Most owners in Port Arthur and across Southeast Texas are doing the books after close, half asleep, hoping nothing slipped through. That is no way to run a kitchen or a business. Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore takes the accounting, payroll, and tax work off your plate completely, so the numbers are handled by people who do this every day while you handle the floor.

## Payroll for restaurants done right, every pay period

Restaurant payroll is its own animal. You have tipped employees, tip credits, and tipped minimum wage rules that have to line up correctly or you risk a wage claim. Overtime stacks up fast during a rush. Turnover means you are onboarding new hires and issuing final checks more often than most businesses ever do. Miss a detail and it shows up as a penalty or an unhappy server.

Our [full-service payroll](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/payroll) handles all of it through a secure online platform. We calculate tipped and standard wages, track overtime, file your payroll taxes, and keep you compliant with the rules that apply to food service employers. New hire this week and a departure next week? We process both without you chasing paperwork.

Your staff gets paid accurately and on time, every period. You get clean records and one less thing to worry about before doors open.

## Tax planning and preparation built around your restaurant's calendar

Tax work for a restaurant should not be a once-a-year scramble. We plan around your year, with quarterly estimates so you are setting money aside before the bill arrives instead of after. That smooths out the cash flow shocks that sink so many food service businesses.

Food service comes with deductions that get missed when someone unfamiliar handles the return. Kitchen equipment and depreciation. Spoiled and wasted inventory. Uniforms and laundry. Smallwares replaced over and over. We know where these live and we claim them. See how our [business tax](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/tax) and [personal income tax](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/personal-income-tax) work fits together for owners who take income from the business.

If the IRS ever sends a notice, you are covered. Our [audit protection](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/audit-protection) means we handle the letters, the responses, and the representation. You forward the envelope and get back to work. The whole experience runs through the same secure digital workflow as the rest of your service, so nothing gets lost between you and your accountant.
