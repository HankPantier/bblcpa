## Where is my refund right now

Most refund questions have the same answer: check the source first. The IRS updates its [Where's My Refund](https://www.irs.gov/refunds) tool once every 24 hours, and it's faster than waiting on hold. Before you click through, have three things on hand.

- Your Social Security number or ITIN
- Your filing status (single, married filing jointly, head of household, etc.)
- The exact refund amount shown on your return, down to the cent

Texas doesn't collect a personal income tax, so there's no state income refund to chase down. If your business is waiting on a sales tax refund, that runs through the [Texas Comptroller's office](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/sales-use/refunds.php) instead, on a separate timeline.

## How long your refund typically takes to arrive

E-filed returns with direct deposit move fastest, usually landing within 21 days of IRS acceptance. Paper returns take longer, often 6 to 8 weeks, because someone has to enter them into the system by hand before processing even starts. Choosing a paper check instead of direct deposit tacks on another week or two at the end.

A few things slow the clock: math errors, a name that doesn't match Social Security records, claims for the Earned Income Tax Credit or Additional Child Tax Credit (these get extra scrutiny by law), or a return flagged for identity verification. None of that means something went wrong on your end. It usually means the IRS is doing exactly what it's supposed to do.

If your refund is running past these windows and you're a BBL client, call us. We prepared your return, so we can tell you in plain terms whether the wait is normal or worth a closer look.

## When to call the IRS versus when to call us

A refund running a few weeks behind schedule usually isn't an emergency. The IRS phone lines stay busy for a reason: most calls are about delays that resolve on their own. If your return shows "still processing" and it's been under 21 days for e-file or 8 weeks for paper, give it more time before you dial.

Call us instead when a notice shows up in the mail, your refund gets offset against a debt you didn't know about, you're asked to verify your identity in person, or a letter mentions an audit. Those moments call for someone who can read the notice, talk to the IRS directly, and respond before a deadline passes.

Our [Audit Protection](/services/outsourced-accounting/audit-protection) service covers exactly this: representation on notices, letters, and proceedings, handled by people who already know your return. You won't be left reading IRS jargon alone at the kitchen table.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Refund tracker | Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore CPAs & Consultants

**Q: How do I check my IRS refund status?**
A: Use the IRS Where's My Refund tool, which updates once every 24 hours. You'll need your Social Security number or ITIN, your filing status, and the exact refund amount from your return. E-filers can check 24 hours after filing; paper filers should wait about four weeks.

**Q: How long does it usually take to get a tax refund?**
A: E-filed returns with direct deposit typically arrive within 21 days of IRS acceptance. Paper returns can take 6 to 8 weeks because they're processed manually. Choosing a paper check instead of direct deposit adds another week or two to either timeline.

**Q: Does Texas have a state income tax refund to track?**
A: No. Texas does not levy a personal income tax, so there's no state income refund process for individuals. Businesses awaiting a sales tax refund work through the Texas Comptroller's office on a separate, unrelated timeline.

**Q: When should I call a CPA instead of the IRS about my refund?**
A: Call a CPA when you receive an IRS notice, your refund is offset against an unexpected debt, you're asked to verify your identity, or a letter mentions an audit. Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore's Audit Protection service handles these situations directly with the IRS.
