## A bigger refund starts before you file

Most people think about their tax refund in April. By then, the decisions that shaped it were made months earlier: how income was timed, what was deducted, which retirement account got funded, and whether the business was structured the right way from the start. Filing is just paperwork at that point. The real work happens in March, July, and October, while there's still time to change the outcome.

For small business owners, physicians, dentists, restaurant operators, and agriculture businesses across Southeast Texas, that distinction matters. A CPA who only shows up at tax time can prepare your return. A CPA who's in the conversation year-round can help you keep more of what you earn. This guide walks through the strategies that make the difference, and how Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore builds them into a fixed-fee relationship instead of a once-a-year scramble.

## The moves that increase what you keep

Strategic tax planning isn't one trick. It's a set of decisions, made at the right time, that compound into real savings. Here's where most of our Southeast Texas clients find room to keep more of their money:

- **Entity structure.** Whether you operate as an LLC, S-corp, or sole proprietorship changes your tax exposure significantly. A restaurant grossing $1.2 million looks very different on paper as an S-corp than as a sole proprietorship, and the right structure can reduce self-employment tax owed.
- **Retirement contributions.** A SEP-IRA or solo 401(k) can shelter a meaningful chunk of business income while building a retirement account at the same time. Physicians and dentists with variable income often have more room here than they realize.
- **Timing income and expenses.** Pushing a December invoice into January, or pulling a planned equipment purchase forward, can shift which tax year absorbs the income or the deduction. This only works if it's planned before December 31, not discovered in April.
- **Deduction tracking that holds up.** Vehicle use, home office space, equipment depreciation, and meals each have specific rules. Missing the documentation costs you the deduction even if the expense was legitimate.
- **Credit card and expense strategy.** Choosing the right business card for rewards and tracking can turn routine spending into cash back or travel credit, with cleaner records for tax time.

None of these require a finance background to use well. They require someone watching your numbers before the calendar runs out.

## Why year-round tax support changes the outcome

Most small business owners in Southeast Texas have had the same experience: a CPA relationship that wakes up in February and goes quiet in May. By the time a planning idea comes up, the deadline to act on it has usually passed.

Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore structures tax support differently. Strategic planning happens throughout the year, not in a single rushed session before April 15. That means an entity change can be evaluated in June instead of discovered as a missed opportunity in March. A retirement contribution can be sized correctly before December instead of estimated after the fact. A slow quarter can trigger an estimated payment adjustment instead of a surprise bill.

Year-round access also means audit support isn't a separate scramble. If a notice arrives from the IRS, representation is already built into the relationship, handled by the team that already knows your books. That continuity, more than any single deduction, is what separates a refund that happens by accident from one that's planned.
