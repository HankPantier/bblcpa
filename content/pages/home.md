## Serving Southeast Texas businesses since 1950

Port Arthur looked different in 1950, when Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore first opened its doors. The refineries were younger, Jefferson County was smaller, and a handful of local business owners needed someone they could trust with the numbers. Seventy-five years later, that's still the job. The firm has guided small business owners, physicians, dentists, restaurant operators, and farmers across Southeast Texas through oil busts, hurricanes, and a tax code that never stops changing.

Longevity like that doesn't happen by accident. It comes from answering the phone, showing up for the audit, and getting the return filed correctly, year after year. Meet the people behind that track record on our [team page](/our-team), or read more about [who we are](/who-we-are).

## One predictable monthly fee covers everything you need

Surprise invoices are stressful, and in accounting they're common. At Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore, outsourced accounting clients pay one fixed monthly fee. No add-on charge when payroll runs an extra cycle. No surprise bill because a tax question took an extra phone call. The scope is set up front, so you know exactly what leaves your account every month, the same way you budget for rent or insurance.

That fee bundles real work, not vague promises. It covers bookkeeping and transaction classification, payroll processing, strategic tax planning and preparation, and access to a secure online platform where you can check your numbers anytime, not just at tax time. Audit protection and year-round support are built into the package too, so a letter from the IRS doesn't turn into a separate bill on top of everything else.

Explore the full [outsourced accounting](https://www.bblcpa.com/services/outsourced-accounting) service to see what's included for your business.

## Real-time financial visibility without the guesswork

Most business owners find out how their company did last quarter three weeks after it mattered. A secure online platform changes that. Log in any time, day or night, and see bookkeeping, payroll, and financial statements as they're updated, not after a phone call and a week of waiting.

That kind of visibility matters most when you're deciding whether to hire, buy equipment, or take a draw. You shouldn't need an accounting degree to read your own numbers, and you shouldn't need an appointment to check them.
