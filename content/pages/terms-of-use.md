## How you may use this website and its content

The articles, guides, e-books, and calculators on this site are here to help small business owners, physicians, dentists, restaurant operators, and individuals across Southeast Texas make smarter financial decisions. You're welcome to read, print, and share our content for your own personal or internal business use.

A few things to keep in mind. Please don't copy, republish, or sell our material as your own, scrape the site with automated tools, or use anything here in a way that breaks the law or interferes with how the site runs. We update content regularly, so figures and guidance can change without notice. If you plan to act on something you read, confirm it with our team first so you're working from current numbers.

## Privacy, data security, and your information

Clients in healthcare, dentistry, and food service hand us some of their most sensitive numbers, and protecting that information is non-negotiable. When you use this site or our secure online platform, we collect only what's needed to deliver your accounting, payroll, and tax services and to keep your account working smoothly.

Your financial data moves through encrypted, password-protected channels, not email attachments or open inboxes. We never sell your information, and we share it only when you ask us to or when the law requires it. The platform gives you real-time visibility into your own books while keeping that access locked to you and your authorized team.

For the full picture of how we gather, store, and handle data, read our [privacy policy](https://www.bblcpa.com/privacy-policy). Have a specific concern? Bring it to us directly and we'll walk you through exactly how your records are protected.

## Disclaimers on accounting and tax information

The general content on this website is meant to inform, not to serve as formal accounting, tax, or legal advice for your specific situation. Tax law shifts, deadlines move, and what's right for a Port Arthur restaurant may be wrong for an agriculture operation down the road. A blog post can't account for the details that make your case yours.

Before you file, restructure, or make a financial decision based on something you found here, talk to a CPA who can look at your full picture. That's what year-round tax support is for. Reading an article is a fine first step. Working with our team turns that information into a plan built around your actual numbers.

We don't control third-party content and can't take responsibility for its accuracy, security, or availability. If a linked tool causes you trouble, that's between you and the provider. When in doubt about whether an outside service fits your needs, ask us before you sign up or share data.
