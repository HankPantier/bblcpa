## Southeast Texas farms and ag operations deserve a CPA who gets it

Farming income rarely arrives in tidy monthly chunks. A crop sells, a herd goes to market, an input bill lands right before planting, and the cash flow chart looks more like a rollercoaster than a steady line. That seasonality is normal for agriculture, and it shapes everything from your tax timing to your equipment purchases.

Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore CPAs & Consultants has worked alongside Southeast Texas businesses since 1950. Over seven decades in Port Arthur, we have learned how commodity price swings, deferred income, and weather-driven uncertainty change the way a farm or ranch should keep its books. You run the operation. We handle the accounting, payroll, and tax work behind it, in plain language and on a schedule that respects your busy seasons.

## Accounting built around how ag businesses make money

General bookkeeping software treats a corn sale and a consulting invoice the same way. Your operation deserves better. We track crop and livestock income separately, so you can see which lines of your business are carrying the load and which are dragging. Equipment depreciation gets handled correctly the first time, including the heavy machinery purchases that drive much of an ag balance sheet.

Input costs, seed, feed, fertilizer, fuel, and labor get categorized so you can compare them season over season instead of guessing. Land transactions, leases, and improvements are recorded with the detail your lender and your future self will both appreciate.

Our [outsourced accounting](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/outsourced-accounting) service bundles all of this into one fixed monthly fee. No hourly meter running every time you call with a question, no surprise invoice after a busy quarter. You know what you pay each month, and you watch your numbers update in real time through a secure online platform. When the banker asks for a current profit and loss statement, it is already there.

<!-- block: checklist-section | variant: with-image | image: harvest-tax-planning.jpg | alt: "Combine harvester working a field during harvest season" | query: "combine harvester field harvest" -->
## Tax planning that works with your harvest schedule, not against it

Ag taxes have their own rulebook, and used well, those rules put real money back in your pocket. We plan year-round, not just in April, so the moves that matter happen while there is still time to make them.

- **Section 179 and bonus depreciation** timed to the years a deduction does the most good, especially after a major equipment purchase
- **Farm income averaging** to smooth out a high-income year against leaner ones and lower your overall tax bill
- **Estimated tax timing** mapped to when your income actually arrives, so you are not overpaying in spring and scrambling in fall
- **Entity structure choices** that reduce self-employment tax and keep more of what you earn
- **Deferred grain and livestock sale strategies** coordinated with your accounting records so the tax treatment matches the transaction

This is the work behind our [tax planning and preparation](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/tax) service. We address the complexity head-on instead of handing you a return in April and hoping for the best. If a notice ever shows up, our [audit protection](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/audit-protection) service puts an experienced CPA between you and the IRS.

## Payroll and compliance handled so you can stay in the field

Ag labor is rarely simple. You might run a small year-round crew and then triple your headcount for harvest, with a mix of W-2 employees and 1099 contractors moving through in a matter of weeks. Each one carries filing requirements, and the deadlines do not pause for planting season.

Our [full-service payroll](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/payroll) management runs through a secure online platform, covering wage calculations, tax withholding, and the year-end W-2 and 1099 paperwork. Seasonal spikes get handled without you chasing forms between rows. You approve, we process, and the compliance side stays clean.
