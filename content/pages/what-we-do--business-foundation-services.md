## Starting a business is exciting, the paperwork, not so much

You've got the idea, the location scouted, maybe a logo already picked out. What you probably haven't sorted out is whether you need an LLC or an S-Corp, what your chart of accounts should look like, or whether a SEP-IRA or a Solo 401(k) makes more sense for a one-person shop. Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore has walked new owners through exactly this since 1950. Three generations of CPAs have watched businesses open their doors across Southeast Texas, and that history tells us which decisions matter on day one and which can wait until month three. Our business foundation services give you a clear starting point, built by people who've set up hundreds of these structures before, not a generic checklist pulled off the internet.

## Why your entity structure decision matters more than you think

Pick the wrong entity type and you'll feel it for years, not weeks. A sole proprietor exposes personal assets to business liability. An S-Corp filed too early adds payroll and reasonable-compensation rules a single-owner shop isn't ready for. An LLC taxed the wrong way can mean self-employment tax on income that didn't need to be taxed that way at all.

This is why entity selection comes before the bank account, before the logo gets printed on anything, before the first invoice goes out. Our [entity type analysis](/what-we-do/entity-type-analysis) walks through liability protection, tax treatment, and what changes if you bring on a partner or employee next year. Restaurants, dental practices, and agriculture operations each carry different risk profiles, and the right structure for a physician's solo practice rarely matches what works for a two-person farm operation. Get this right first, and everything else, the books, the payroll, the tax filing, gets simpler.

## Get your books right from day one

Most new owners don't think about bookkeeping until April, then scramble to reconstruct a year of transactions from bank statements and memory. We set up your accounting system before you need it, not after.

Your books, payroll, and reporting run through one secure online platform, so you can check cash position, payables, and revenue in real time instead of waiting on a quarterly call. Because everything is bundled into a fixed monthly fee, growth doesn't trigger a surprise bill. Add an employee, open a second location, or start accepting credit cards, your fee stays predictable while we adjust the work behind it. That's the difference between bookkeeping built for a startup and bookkeeping inherited from a spreadsheet nobody updated since launch day.
