## Tax planning guides for Southeast Texas business owners

Tax planning works best when it speaks your industry's language. A few starting points for the businesses we see most around Port Arthur.

**Medical and dental practices.** Equipment, continuing education, malpractice premiums, and practice-related travel can all reduce taxable income, but the rules on timing and documentation are strict. Buying a new imaging system in December versus January changes your deduction year and your cash position. We help physicians and dentists time major purchases against their actual revenue picture.

**Restaurants.** Tip reporting and payroll taxes trip up more owners than anything else. Allocated tips, the FICA tip credit, and accurate Form 8027 reporting all affect what you owe and what you can claim back. Get the payroll side right and you stop overpaying without inviting IRS attention.

**Agriculture.** Schedule F gives farm and ranch operations room for income averaging, deductions on equipment and feed, and depreciation choices that flatten out good years and bad. Weather and commodity prices already make income lumpy. Smart planning keeps your tax bill from making it lumpier.

**General small business.** Entity-level deductions, Section 179 expensing, and the qualified business income deduction shift real dollars depending on how your business is structured. The right [entity choice](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/entity-type-analysis) often matters more than any single write-off.

These are starting points, not a substitute for a plan built on your numbers. Our [tax team](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/tax) builds that plan year-round, not just in April.

## Bookkeeping and payroll basics every small business owner should know

Clean books are not complicated, they are just consistent. The owners who stay out of trouble do a few simple things every month.

Keep business and personal money in separate accounts. One business checking account and one business card draws a clean line that an auditor, a lender, or your future self will thank you for. Mixing the two is the fastest way to lose deductions and create confusion at tax time.

Record transactions while they are fresh. Categorizing income and expenses weekly beats a shoebox marathon every quarter. When the books are current, you actually know whether you can afford that hire or that equipment.

Run payroll the right way the first time. Misclassifying an employee as a contractor, missing a payroll tax deposit, or filing 1099s late are the mistakes that create real IRS exposure. Our [payroll service](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/payroll) handles withholding, filings, and deadlines so none of that lands on you.

Reconcile every month. Matching your books to your bank statement catches errors and missing entries before they snowball. None of this requires an accounting degree. It requires a system and someone to keep it running. That is what our [bookkeeping team](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/accounting) does.

## Starting a business in Texas: what to set up from day one

The first few decisions you make shape your taxes, your liability, and your paperwork for years. Get these right at launch and you avoid expensive corrections later.

- **Choose the right entity.** Sole proprietorship, LLC, S corp, or C corp each carry different tax and liability consequences. Our [entity type analysis](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/entity-type-analysis) matches the structure to how you actually plan to operate and pay yourself.
- **Set up your accounting system correctly.** A clean chart of accounts from day one beats untangling a mess in year two. Our [accounting system setup](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/accounting-system-setup) gets your books organized before the first transaction.
- **Register as an employer.** If you plan to hire, you need an EIN, state employer registration, and payroll set up before the first paycheck. Missing a deposit deadline in month one is a painful way to start.
- **Plan for retirement early.** A SEP IRA or solo 401(k) opened in year one compounds for decades. Our [retirement plan analysis](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/retirement-plan-analysis) compares your options against your income.

These four moves are the backbone of our [Business Foundation Services](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/business-foundation-services), built for owners launching in Southeast Texas.

## What to do if you receive an IRS notice

First, breathe. Most IRS letters are routine. A CP2000 means the income reported under your number does not match your return. A CP14 is a balance-due notice. A request for documentation is exactly that, a request, not an accusation.

Here is what not to do: do not ignore it, and do not call the IRS and start explaining off the top of your head. Deadlines on these notices are real, and anything you say becomes part of the record.

Forward the notice to us instead. With [Audit Protection](https://www.bblcpa.com/what-we-do/audit-protection), we handle the correspondence, the deadlines, and the conversations with the IRS on your behalf. You stay focused on your business while we sort out what the letter actually requires and respond correctly the first time.
