## Your spending should be working harder for you

You swipe the company card a dozen times a day. Fuel, supplies, software, a working lunch with a vendor. Every one of those purchases could be earning cash back, points, or travel credit, and for most business owners, that money quietly slips away. The card someone signed up for years ago rarely matches how the business spends today. That is a real leak, not a rounding error. A CPA who already sees your books is positioned to catch it. At Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore, we treat your credit card strategy as part of your financial picture, not an afterthought. Small choices, made on purpose, add up across a year.

<!-- block: process-steps | variant: vertical -->
## How BBL's credit card rewards advisory works

This is advisor-led guidance from the firm that keeps your books, not a pitch for a card we get paid to sell.

### We map your spending categories
Using the numbers already in your accounting records, we group your purchases by category, so the comparison reflects how your business operates instead of a one-size-fits-all profile.

### We compare the card options
We research current business cards across rewards structures, annual fees, sign-up bonuses, and redemption rules, then narrow the field to the few that genuinely fit your numbers.

### We recommend and explain the trade-offs
You get a clear recommendation with the reasoning behind it. No commissions, no quotas, no product we are paid to push. Just a straight answer about which card returns the most for the way you spend, and what each one costs you to carry.

## Rewards that fit how your business spends

A restaurant owner's card statement looks nothing like a physician's, and neither one resembles a farm operation in Southeast Texas. The restaurant runs heavy on food costs, supplies, and utilities. The dental practice spends on lab work, equipment, and staffing. The farm has fuel, feed, and seasonal swings that spike in spring and quiet down by winter.

A generic "best rewards card" list ignores all of that. The card that earns the most for a clinic with steady monthly software and payroll charges may return almost nothing for a grower whose biggest outlays land in two months. We build the recommendation around your categories, your typical monthly volume, and your goals, whether that is cash back to reinvest or travel credit you will actually use. The point is a card that pays you for the spending you were already doing.

## Why your CPA is the right person to give this advice

Credit card strategy is not a standalone topic. It touches your taxes, your bookkeeping, and your monthly close. A bank or a points blog cannot see any of that. We can.

The annual fee on a business card is generally deductible, which changes the real math on whether a premium card earns its keep. The card you choose also feeds straight into your accounting system, so a clean rewards structure and good digital statements keep your books tidy, while a messy one creates reconciliation headaches every month. We know which cards integrate cleanly because we are the ones reconciling them.

Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore has worked with small businesses across Southeast Texas since 1950. Seven decades of bookkeeping for restaurants, medical practices, farms, and new owners means we have seen which card choices help and which quietly cause problems at tax time.
