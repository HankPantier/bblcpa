## Tax season handled from start to finish

Business tax planning and preparation at Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore covers both ends of the calendar: the strategy that happens months before a number ever lands on a form, and the filing itself. Everything moves through a secure online platform, so you upload documents, review your return, and message your CPA without driving across Port Arthur or printing a single page.

This isn't a service assembled for filing season and forgotten the rest of the year. BBL has prepared returns and built tax strategy for Southeast Texas businesses since 1950, and that history shapes how the firm handles deadlines, deductions, and IRS correspondence today. You get a plan built around your business, not a generic checklist recycled from the last client.

No shoebox of receipts. No waiting on hold. Just a clear process and a team that already knows how Texas businesses get taxed.

## Year-round tax strategy so nothing catches you off guard

Filing a return once a year tells you what already happened. It doesn't change the outcome. BBL's tax service is built around quarterly check-ins and ongoing planning, so estimated payments, entity decisions, and deduction opportunities get addressed while there's still time to act on them, not in March when the prior year is already locked in.

That means a conversation about equipment purchases before year-end, not after. A heads-up on a quarterly estimate before the penalty hits. A second look at your entity structure if your revenue has shifted enough to matter.

This level of attention is already part of the fixed monthly fee, not an add-on billed by the hour every time you call with a question. You won't get a surprise invoice for a fifteen-minute conversation about a 1099 question in October. The planning and the filing are part of the same bundled service, which is the whole point of working with a firm instead of software.

## How our tax process works

Getting started doesn't require a stack of paperwork on day one. Here's what the relationship looks like once you're on board.

### Onboarding
You meet with a CPA to walk through your business structure, prior returns, and the goals you have for the year ahead.

### Secure document upload
Tax documents, receipts, and statements get uploaded through the online platform instead of emailed back and forth or dropped off in person.

### Review and strategy
Your CPA reviews everything against current tax law and flags opportunities, whether that's a deduction you missed last year or a structural change worth considering.

### Filing
Once the strategy is set, your return gets prepared, reviewed internally, and filed, with you signing off digitally before anything goes to the IRS.

### Ongoing communication
The relationship doesn't end at filing. Quarterly check-ins and year-round access to your CPA keep planning ahead of deadlines instead of behind them.

An IRS letter shows up and most business owners feel their stomach drop a little, even when it's routine. With Audit Protection included, that letter goes straight to your CPA instead of sitting on your desk while you decide what it means.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Business tax planning and preparation | Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore CPAs & Consultants

**Q: What does business tax planning and preparation include at BBL?**
A: It includes year-round strategic planning plus the actual filing of your business return, delivered through a secure online platform. Clients get quarterly check-ins, proactive deduction and entity guidance, and a fixed monthly fee that covers both the planning and the preparation work.

**Q: Is tax planning only available during tax season?**
A: No. BBL's model is built around year-round strategy, not a once-a-year filing rush. Quarterly check-ins and ongoing access to your CPA mean decisions that affect your tax bill get addressed while you can still act on them, not after the year closes.

**Q: What happens if I get an IRS notice?**
A: Audit Protection is included with BBL's tax service, meaning notices, letters, and IRS proceedings are handled directly by your CPA at no additional cost. You forward the letter and the firm takes it from there.

**Q: Does tax strategy differ by industry?**
A: Yes. Physicians, dentists, restaurants, and agriculture businesses each face different depreciation rules, entity considerations, and deduction opportunities. BBL tailors planning to the specific industry, drawing on decades of experience with these niches across Southeast Texas.
