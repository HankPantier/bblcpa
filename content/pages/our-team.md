## Seven decades of trusted accounting in Southeast Texas

Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore opened its doors in Port Arthur in 1950, back when a handshake and a ledger book were standard equipment. Three names, one firm, and a habit of sticking around long after other shops have come and gone. Seventy-five years later, the ledgers are digital and the handshake is optional, but the people behind the numbers still live and work in Southeast Texas. Meet the team that's been guiding physicians, restaurant owners, dentists, farmers, and small business owners through tax season, payroll, and everything in between since Truman was in the White House.

<!-- block: team-grid | variant: 3-col -->
## The people guiding your financial future

### Robert Brammer, CPA
Managing Partner

Robert has spent over 30 years untangling the books for restaurant owners and physicians across Jefferson County. He still does the math on a legal pad before he trusts the spreadsheet, and clients say that's exactly why they trust him.

### Denise Begnaud, CPA, CGMA
Partner, Tax Strategy

Denise leads the firm's tax planning work, with a particular focus on entity structuring for new business owners. She's walked dozens of Port Arthur startups through the LLC-versus-S-corp decision before they ever filed their first return.

### Michael Lattimore, CPA
Partner, Audit & Assurance

Michael handles audit and assurance engagements for the firm's nonprofit and agriculture clients, a niche he picked up growing up on a rice farm outside Beaumont. He knows the difference between a bad harvest and bad bookkeeping, and he can usually tell you which one you're dealing with.

### Sarah Thibodeaux, CPA
Senior Accountant, Healthcare & Dental Practices

Sarah works almost exclusively with physicians and dentists, helping them manage payroll for clinical staff and plan around the cash-flow swings that come with insurance reimbursements. She's been with the firm for 12 years.

### James Fontenot, CPA
Manager, Payroll & Small Business Services

James runs the day-to-day payroll operations for dozens of small businesses across Southeast Texas, from food trucks to family-owned retail shops. He's the one who answers when a client calls in a mild panic the morning before payday.

## How our team serves Southeast Texas businesses

A general accountant can balance a ledger. Our team knows why a restaurant's food costs spike in August, why a dental practice's cash flow lags behind its production numbers, and why an agriculture operation needs its books to match the planting calendar, not the calendar year. That kind of knowledge doesn't come from a textbook. It comes from Michael working a rice farm, Sarah sitting across the table from dentists for over a decade, and Robert reading restaurant P&Ls since the Carter administration.

Clients in [physicians](/industries/physicians), [dentists](/industries/dentists), [restaurants](/industries/restaurants), [agriculture](/industries/agriculture), and [small business owners](/industries/small-business-owners) get a team that already understands their numbers before the first meeting ends. That's the difference between a firm that processes your data and one that actually reads it.

## Fixed fees, real-time access, and answers all year

Working with our team doesn't start and stop at tax season, and it doesn't come with a surprise invoice at the end of the month. Here's what clients can count on:
