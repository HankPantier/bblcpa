## Your business took decades to build, your exit deserves the same care

You spent years getting the hours, the payroll, and the cash flow right. The day you hand the business off deserves that same level of attention, planned on your schedule rather than forced by a health scare or a surprise offer.

Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore has watched Port Arthur businesses change hands since 1950. Over seven decades in Southeast Texas, we have seen owners who started early keep more of what they built, and owners who waited leave money and options on the table. Succession planning is not a sign something is wrong. It is the same forward thinking that got you here. The earlier you map the route, the more choices you keep, whether you plan to sell, bring in family, or step back gradually over a few years.

## What a CPA brings to your succession plan that an attorney alone cannot

An attorney drafts the buy-sell agreement and the legal documents that move ownership. That work matters. But a transfer also lives in numbers, and that is where a CPA does the heavy lifting.

Four pieces sit squarely in our domain. Entity structure decides how a sale or transfer is taxed, and the right [entity type analysis](https://www.bblcpa.com/services/business-foundation-services/entity-type-analysis) before a deal can change your outcome by six figures. Valuation grounds the conversation in real financials instead of a guess. Tax-efficient transfer strategy decides whether you sell outright, gift shares over time, or structure an installment plan. And cash flow planning makes sure the business, and you, stay solvent through the handoff.

Think of us as the financial quarterback for business owners across Port Arthur and Jefferson County. We coordinate with your attorney and your financial advisor so every number lines up before anyone signs. Our [tax planning team](https://www.bblcpa.com/services/outsourced-accounting/tax) models the strategies side by side, so you choose with the full picture in front of you rather than after the fact.

## Succession planning looks different depending on your industry

The broad strokes are the same. The details are not. A dental practice does not change hands the way a rice farm does, and the tax and valuation work follows the difference.

### Physicians
icon: Stethoscope

Practice value ties to patient panels, referral relationships, and payer contracts that do not transfer automatically. We help [physicians](/industries/physicians) value the practice realistically and plan the income transition so retirement does not arrive with a tax shock.

### Dentists
icon: HeartPulse

Equipment, real estate, and goodwill each carry different tax treatment in a sale. For [dentists](/industries/dentists), we separate those pieces so the buyer and seller agree on what the practice is actually worth and how the proceeds are taxed.

### Restaurants
icon: Building

Brand, lease terms, and equipment condition drive a restaurant sale more than last year's revenue. We help [restaurant owners](/industries/restaurants) clean up the books and clarify what conveys, so a lease assignment or franchise transfer does not stall the deal.

### Agriculture
icon: Home

Land and equipment often hold decades of appreciation, which means capital gains and estate questions sit at the center. We help [agriculture families](/industries/agriculture) plan transfers that keep the operation intact across generations.

### Small business owners
icon: Briefcase

Many owners are the business. We help [small business owners](/industries/small-business-owners) document systems, stabilize cash flow, and build a value that survives your departure rather than walking out the door with you.

## The tax side of transferring your business is where most owners get surprised

Every exit path carries a tax bill, and the size of it depends almost entirely on planning you do years ahead. Here is the plain-language version.

An outright sale usually triggers capital gains, and how the price splits between assets and goodwill changes what you owe. A family transfer raises gift and estate questions, where lifetime exemptions and gradual gifting can shrink the tax over several years. An employee or management buyout often runs on installment payments, which spreads income, and the tax, across the years you receive it. Each route has a different best-case structure, and the wrong one can cost more than the deal itself.

This is the heart of business succession tax planning in Port Arthur. Start early and you have room to shift entity structure, time the transfer, and use the exemptions available to you. Wait until a buyer is at the table and most of those levers are already gone. We pair tax projections with [entity type analysis](https://www.bblcpa.com/services/business-foundation-services/entity-type-analysis) so you see the after-tax number on each path before you commit, not in April after the closing.
