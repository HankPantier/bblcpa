## What the dependent care tax credit covers

Summer camp bills add up fast, but here's some good news: the dependent care tax credit isn't limited to daycare. If you're a working parent in Port Arthur, Beaumont, or anywhere else in Southeast Texas paying for day camp this summer, those costs may qualify for a real reduction in your tax bill.

The credit was built for work-related care, the kind that lets parents keep a job running while their kids under 13 are looked after. Camp tuition counts the same way daycare does, whether it's a sports camp, an art camp, or a week of coding camp, as long as the underlying purpose is care while you work.

<!-- block: checklist-section | variant: with-image | image: parent-checking-camp-list.jpg | alt: "Parent reviewing a checklist of summer camp options on a tablet" | query: "parent reviewing checklist tablet" -->
## Which camps qualify and which ones don't

The IRS draws clear lines here, and knowing them ahead of time saves a headache in April. Before you assume your summer plans qualify, check the rules:

- Day camps qualify, including specialty camps for sports, art, or academics
- Overnight camps do not qualify, no exceptions, regardless of cost
- The child must be under age 13 when the care is provided
- The camp expense has to enable you (and your spouse, if married) to work or actively look for work
- If you're married, both spouses generally need earned income for the year
- Educational tutoring or summer school tuition doesn't count, only care-based programs

Get this part wrong and you could overstate a credit you can't actually claim. Get it right, and you've got a documented break sitting in your tax return.

## How much you could save

The credit isn't a flat dollar amount. It's a percentage of eligible expenses, capped based on how many kids you're claiming care for, and the percentage depends on your income.

| Number of children | Eligible expense cap | Credit rate | Potential credit |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 child | $3,000 | 20% to 35% | $600 to $1,050 |
| 2 or more children | $6,000 | 20% to 35% | $1,200 to $2,100 |

Families with adjusted gross income of $43,000 or less get the top 35% rate. The rate steps down as income rises, landing at 20% for most middle- and higher-income households. Either way, it's a direct credit against tax owed, not just a deduction, so the savings land dollar for dollar.

## What you need to claim the credit

The IRS wants proof, not just a number you remember from August. Start a folder now and you won't be hunting for this in March:

- Camp provider's legal name and address
- The camp's Employer Identification Number (EIN) or, for an individual provider, their Social Security number
- Total amount paid for the summer, broken out by child if you used more than one camp
- Receipts or invoices showing dates of care
- Form 2441, which is filed alongside your federal return

Most camps will hand you a year-end statement with this information already organized. Ask before the season ends, not after.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Summer camps may qualify for the dependent care tax credit | Brammer, Begnaud, & Lattimore CPAs & Consultants

**Q: Does overnight camp qualify for the dependent care tax credit?**
A: No. The IRS only allows day camp expenses, not overnight camp, to count toward the dependent care credit. The distinction applies regardless of cost or camp type, so confirm with your camp provider whether it's a day or residential program before assuming the expense qualifies.

**Q: How much is the dependent care tax credit worth for summer camp?**
A: Eligible expenses cap at $3,000 for one child or $6,000 for two or more children. The credit rate ranges from 20% to 35% based on income, meaning a potential credit of $600 to $1,050 for one child, or $1,200 to $2,100 for two or more.

**Q: What documentation do I need to claim camp expenses on my taxes?**
A: You'll need the camp provider's name, address, and EIN (or Social Security number for an individual provider), total amounts paid, dates of care, and Form 2441 filed with your federal return. Most camps provide a year-end summary with this information.

**Q: Can both parents claim the dependent care credit if they're married?**
A: Generally, both spouses need earned income during the year for the expense to qualify, and the credit is claimed jointly on a married filing jointly return. Married couples filing separately typically cannot claim this credit.
