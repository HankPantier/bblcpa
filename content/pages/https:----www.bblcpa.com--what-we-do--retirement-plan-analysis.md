## The right retirement plan can save you money now and later

A good retirement plan does two jobs at once. It lowers your tax bill this year, and it builds wealth you can actually retire on. Plenty of business owners miss one or both, usually because they set up a plan years ago and never looked at it again, or never set one up at all.

That is money left on the table. The contribution limits, the deduction rules, and the right structure for a physician with three employees look nothing like the right structure for a restaurant owner running solo. If your plan was a default choice from a decade ago, it may be costing you more than it saves.

Our retirement plan analysis fixes that. We look at where you are now, what you want later, and which plan moves you toward both. Then we hand you a clear recommendation, not a stack of brochures.

## Not all retirement plans are built the same

There is no single best retirement plan. There are several good ones, and which one fits depends on your income, your entity type, and how many people you employ. Here is the plain-language version.

A SEP-IRA is simple to run and lets you contribute a healthy percentage of income, which suits owners with few or no employees. A SIMPLE IRA works well for small teams that want a modest match without heavy administration. A Solo 401(k) is built for owner-only businesses and often allows the largest contributions at moderate income levels. A defined benefit plan, closer to an old-fashioned pension, can shelter much larger amounts for high-earning owners who want to catch up fast.

Each one carries tradeoffs: contribution caps, paperwork, employee coverage rules, and how much flexibility you get year to year. A high-earning dentist nearing retirement has very different math than a new business owner just turning a profit. The wrong plan caps your savings or buries you in administration. The right one does the opposite.

## How BBL researches and compares your options

We do the legwork so you do not have to read the fine print on five different plans. Here is how the analysis works.

### We gather the details that matter

We start with your business entity, your owner income, your number of employees, and your goals. A sole proprietor planning to hire next year needs a different recommendation than an established partnership.

### We model the contribution math

For each viable plan, we calculate what you could contribute, what you could deduct, and what it would cost to administer. Real numbers, based on your books, not generic examples.

### We compare options side by side

You get a clear comparison of the plans that actually fit, with the tradeoffs spelled out in plain language. Contribution limits, employee impact, flexibility, and paperwork, all in one view.

### We give you a recommendation you can act on

We tell you which plan we would choose in your position and why. If you want to move forward, we help you set it up and keep it on track year after year.
